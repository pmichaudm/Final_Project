Sushi Facts

Sushi dates as far back as 2nd Century BC in China, which was known as a dish called “narezushi”. Sushi that we are more familiar with today was originally a method to preserve fish in China in order to store fish for extended periods withou refrigeration. Japanese people started eating the preserved raw fish as is, much to the initial confusion from Chinese people.

In the beginning, sushi was eaten by hand, although nowadays there is no incorrect way to eat it, whether it be by hand, chopsticks - or even with a spoon!

At first, it was unheard of in Japan to eat raw salmon, however when Norwegians came up with the concept of salmon sushi and assuring that Atlantic salmon was safe to eat raw in comparison to Pacific salmon, Japan started integrating raw salmon with their sushi. Because of this, most salmon sushi comes from fish that are raised on farms rather than in the wild.

In order to kill parasites before consumption, all fish that is to be eaten raw must be frozen prior to consumption

The highest price ever paid for a sushi grade Bluefin Tuna was $396,000 for a 754 lbs. at the Tsukiji Fish Market in Tokyo on January 4th, 2011.
